## What Is "The Wheel" and Why Do I Keep Talking About It?

If you're new here, the wheel is the simplest options income strategy that exists. It works like this:

1. **Sell a cash-secured put** at a strike price you'd be happy owning the stock at
2. If the stock stays above your strike, you keep the premium. Free money. Go back to step 1
3. If the stock drops below your strike, you get assigned. Now you own shares. That's fine, you wanted them
4. **Sell a covered call** at a strike price you'd be happy selling at
5. If the stock stays below your strike, you keep the premium. Go back to step 4
6. If the stock rises above your strike, your shares get called away. You pocket the gains. Go back to step 1

That's it. That's the whole thing. You're getting paid to buy low and sell high. The premiums you collect along the way lower your cost basis until your break-even price is so low that the stock would have to absolutely crater for you to lose money.

I call this your **True Basis**. Not what you paid for the stock. What you paid minus every single dollar of premium you've collected along the way.
