## Enter the 7/2/1 Rule

The standard options wheel strategy is boring. It works, but selling covered calls on Ford or AT&T is a phenomenal way to watch paint dry while tying up your capital for a 4% annualized return. 

If you want to actually build an account, you need volatility. You need the fresh meat. 

I am not talking about trading LUNR or ASTS today. I am talking about finding those companies *before* they hit the front page of Reddit and every financial newsletter in the country. You want to fight for the entries right when the options chains are just starting to wake up.

That is the 7/2/1 rule. 

*   **Market Cap:** $50M to $1B. Big enough to be real, small enough to move.
*   **Options Chain:** Active or just waking up. We want high implied volatility. 
*   **Catalyst:** A real narrative. Semiconductors, SaaS, or structural shifts. 
*   **Volume:** Relative volume spiking over 1.5x. Institutional footprints.

If you just run a generic "small cap options" screen on FinViz, you are going to get trapped. You will pull up biotech companies doing reverse splits or buyout targets where the option premium is completely dead. We do not want those.
