Prologue
The System

"Day trading doesn't need to be as hard as people make it. You just need to know what to trade, when to buy, and when to walk away."

This is the diary of a simple system. Every morning at 5 AM, a screener scans 72 stocks — the ones that have 2x leveraged ETFs — and finds which ones are actually trending. Not "maybe trending." Not "could be trending if you squint." Actually trending, with the ADX to prove it and the volume to back it up.

Three rules govern everything:

🚦

Rule 1: Is the Market Trending?

Check SPY's 1-hour ADX. If it's under 20, the market is chopping — don't trade. This single rule saved us from $1,167 in losses across 2 weeks.

⏰

Rule 2: Buy the 10 AM Dip

2x ETFs dip an average of -1.9% in the first 30 minutes. Wait for it. Enter at 10:00 AM ET. This adds +$1,168 vs. buying at open.

🎯

Rule 3: Let the ATR Ride

Set a trailing stop at 1× the daily ATR. Don't touch it. Don't get scared. Don't get greedy. Let the math work.

Position sizing: $9,000 on the #1 conviction pick, $4,500 each on #2 and #3. That's $18K deployed, $9K cash buffer. Starting capital: $27,000. Goal: get to $30,000, withdraw the $3K, start over.

The Cycle

$27,000 → trade → $30,000 → withdraw $3,000 → $27,000 → repeat. That's $3K/week income if the system works. Let's see if it does.

Honest Limitations

This is 2 weeks of data — 10 trading days, 6 active. Not enough to prove anything. Spreads, slippage, and commissions are not included. The ATR trailing stop models realistic exits but isn't perfect. Past performance is not future performance. But the logic is sound, the safeguards work, and the entry timing edge is structural — 2x ETFs will dip in the first 30 minutes because of how leveraged rebalancing mechanics work.
