## The 35-Stock Portfolio Strategy

This strategy utilizes a 60/40 allocation model, spread across 35 carefully selected stocks. To keep the math clean and the rebalancing rules simple, the portfolio operates on a strict 2:1 weighting ratio.

*   **The Core (60%):** 15 high-conviction stocks, each weighted at 4%.
*   **The Satellites (40%):** 20 growth or exploratory stocks, each weighted at 2%.

## The Rebalancing Philosophy

The hardest part of portfolio management is knowing when to let your runners run and when to stop watering your weeds. This strategy removes the emotion from that decision.

Instead of rebalancing based on an arbitrary calendar date or minor market noise, the trigger is entirely price driven. A rebalance is only considered when a 2% stock outgrows a 4% core stock in pure dollar value.

### Why a 2:1 Ratio?

In a standard $10,000 portfolio, your 4% positions represent $400 and your 2% positions represent $200. For the smaller position to overtake the larger one, two significant events must happen simultaneously:

1.  The 2% stock must gain 40% (growing from $200 to $280).
2.  The 4% stock must drop 30% (falling from $400 to $280).

You could technically use a 3:2 weighting ratio (like 6% and 4%), where a 20% gain and a 20% drop would trigger a rebalance. However, a 20% swing is often just normal sector rotation or market noise. Requiring a +40% and -30% collision ensures you are only stepping in when there is a meaningful, fundamental divergence.

## The Math: Drawdowns & Recoveries

When evaluating these rebalancing triggers, it is crucial to understand the asymmetrical nature of losses and gains.

### The Recovery Climb

When a stock drops, it requires a significantly higher percentage gain just to get back to breakeven. This highlights why letting a stock drop 30% is a critical threshold:

*   A -10% loss requires an 11.1% recovery.
*   A -20% loss requires a 25% recovery.
*   A -30% loss requires a 43% recovery.
*   A -40% loss requires a 67% recovery.

![Drawdowns and Recoveries](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/mphinance/mphinance/main/docs/substack/musings/drawdowns_and_recoveries.png)

### The Pullback Buffer

Conversely, if your runners are moving up, here is the percentage drop they can stomach before giving up their initial gains:

*   A 10% gain is wiped out by a 9% drop.
*   A 20% gain is wiped out by a 17% drop.
*   A 30% gain is wiped out by a 23% drop.
*   A 40% gain is wiped out by a 28% drop.

By locking in the +40% / -30% parameters, you give your winners the exact room they need to breathe while keeping a tight, mathematical leash on the underperformers.
