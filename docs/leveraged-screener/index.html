02 Strategy Rules

📡

Pre-Market Screening (5 AM)

Scan 72 underlying stocks using 1H ADX ≥ 15, prior-day relative volume ≥ 1.2×, and DI+/DI- direction. Map to highest-volume 2x bull or bear ETF.

🚦

SPY Regime Filter

ADX ≥ 20 = GO — trending market, take all trades.
ADX 15–20 = CAUTION — sit out.
ADX < 15 = STOP — chop zone, no trades.

📊

Signal Count Cap

If > 25 signals fire on a single day, everything is moving — that's chop, not trend. Sit out. This filter alone prevented a losing day.

⏰

Entry at 10:00 AM ET

Wait 30 minutes after open for the "morning dip" to complete. Backtested 12 entry times — 10:00 AM was optimal, adding +$1,168 vs. buying at open.

🎯

1× Daily ATR Trailing Stop

Trail from peak using 1× the 14-period daily ATR. Tested 8 exit methods — ATR trail maximized P&L. Tighter stops get shaken out on 2x ETF intraday volatility.

💰

Conviction Sizing

A-Grade (#1 ADX): $9,000 (33%)
B-Grade (#2–3): $4,500 each (17%)

08 Methodology

Data Sources

  • Screening: TradingView scanner API — 1H ADX, RSI, DI+/DI-, relative volume
  • Backtest pricing: Yahoo Finance 5-minute intraday bars, daily OHLCV for ATR
  • Regime data: SPY 1H candles with 14-period ADX

ETF Universe

72 underlying stocks mapped to 2x leveraged bull and bear ETFs across 4 providers: Direxion, GraniteShares, REX Shares, and Defiance ETFs. Resolution uses volume-weighted selection when multiple ETFs exist for the same underlying.

Profit Calculation

All P&L uses ATR trailing stop simulation — not max favorable excursion (MFE). The simulator walks each 5-minute bar, tracks the peak price, and triggers exit when the price reverses by 1× the 14-period daily ATR from its high. This models realistic trailing stop behavior including slippage and timing.

What This Backtest Does NOT Account For

  • Spread/slippage (2x ETFs typically have 1-3¢ spreads)
  • Commission (varies by broker)
  • Days with no valid signals (treated as sit-outs)
  • Black swan events or circuit breakers
  • Sample size is 2 weeks (10 trading days, 6 active) — longer validation recommended
