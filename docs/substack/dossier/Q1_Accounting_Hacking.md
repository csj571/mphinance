The thesis: Speed belongs to the machines. Governance belongs to the humans.

Most people use AI like a magic 8-ball. I use it like a jury.

One model is a guess. Ten models is a verdict. Here is the hierarchy:

- **2 Models:** A glorified spell check.
- **3 Models:** A tie-breaker for basic logic.
- **6 Models:** A full system audit.
- **10 Models:** A consensus so tight that hallucination becomes statistically impossible.

Instead of trusting one autonomous model, you query a constellation of them in parallel: Claude, GPT, Gemini, Grok, all of them. If two models agree, it might be a coincidence. If ten different architectures arrive at the same conclusion, you have consensus.

But the real money glitch is what he calls **Catfish Lane.** It's a mandatory mechanism that forces one specific model to violently dissent against the others, purely to stress-test their logic. It's an automated, permanent Red Team. It kills groupthink before it starts.
